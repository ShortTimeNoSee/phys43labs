1. Single Lens (Focal Lengths)

1/do + 1/di = 1/f
Bench Setup: Place the light source at the 0 mm mark. Place the lens at a known distance (do). Move the viewing screen back and forth until the image is in sharp focus, and record the screen's distance from the lens (di). Repeat for at least 5 different positions. The linear regression of 1/di vs 1/do will isolate the focal length.
1/di = -(1/do) + 1/f
Linear Equation Form (y = mx + b)

3. Multiple Lenses: Microscope

Mtotal = m1 × m2
Bench Setup: Place the Objective Lens (B) in the middle of the track. Put the light source exactly 15 mm away (do1). Move the screen to find the sharp image (this distance is di1). Then, place the Eyepiece (A) behind the screen and focus it. The intermediate image on the screen acts as the object for the Eyepiece.
